Touch Events Touch events are triggered when a touch-sensitive surface is touched at one or more points. When a touch surface is touched, the point of contact between the finger and the screen is known as the touch point. Until recently, these touch events usually indicated that the device in use was a mobile device, such as a smartphone or tablet. However, increasingly, laptops or desktops may have touch-sensitive surfaces in the form of a trackpad or touch screen.1

TouchLists and Touch Objects The three unique touch properties of the TouchEvent object all contain TouchList collections, which contain individual Touch objects. TouchList collections have a length property that indicates how many Touch objects are in the collection. Devices that support multiple touch points can have more than one Touch object in these collections. The individual Touch objects contain useful information about the touch point.
